\begin{abstract}
  This work presents NearestAP, a distributed leader election protocol designed for clusters
  operating over unreliable, connectionless broadcast media. The protocol enables a group of
  autonomous drones to dynamically agree on a leader based on locally computed node potential,
  even under extreme message loss. NearestAP avoids connection-oriented communication and instead
  relies on probabilistic information dissemination, adaptive election timing, and silence-driven
  recovery mechanisms. Experimental evaluation in a simulated environment shows successful
  convergence even when up to 90\% of messages are lost per node, making the protocol
  particularly suitable for embedded systems operating over non-conventional buses.
\end{abstract}
